Receiving a claim denial can be frustrating, especially when you know your purchase qualifies. The good news is that most denials can be resolved. This article walks you through the most common reasons claims are denied, how to appeal, and when Truemed can step in to help.

For a detailed breakdown of specific denial reasons and targeted fixes, see [Why Was My Claim Denied?](/reimbursements-and-claims/why-was-my-claim-denied).

---

## Common denial reasons

HSA and FSA administrators deny claims for a handful of recurring reasons. Understanding which category your denial falls into is the first step toward resolving it.

**Document issues**
The most common cause of denials is missing or incomplete documentation. This includes:
- An LMN (Letter of Medical Necessity) that is missing a signature
- Provider credentials that don't meet your administrator's requirements (for example, some administrators only accept LMNs signed by an MD, not a nurse practitioner)
- A provider address in a different state than your own, which some administrators flag
- Receipts that are not itemized or don't include all required merchant information

**Product ineligibility**
Your administrator may determine that the product or service doesn't meet their eligibility criteria. This often happens when:
- The purchase is considered preventive only, rather than for treatment of a diagnosed condition
- The diagnosis on the LMN doesn't clearly connect to the product purchased
- The item falls into a category that requires stronger medical necessity documentation (such as a mattress or sauna)

**Administrator-specific requirements not met**
Each administrator has its own rules and review processes. A claim may be denied simply because a specific requirement unique to your plan wasn't satisfied, even if the purchase itself is eligible.

**Incomplete documentation overall**
Claims can also be denied when required documentation is present but incomplete, such as an LMN that lacks a clinical rationale, a receipt that doesn't show the purchase date, or missing provider contact information.

## How to appeal a denied claim

Appealing a denied claim is a straightforward process. Follow these steps to give your appeal the best chance of success.

**Step 1: Read the denial notice carefully**
Your denial notice will include a reason code or written explanation. Identify exactly why the claim was denied before taking any action. This determines what documentation you need to gather.

**Step 2: Gather the right documentation**
Based on the denial reason, collect everything needed to address it. This might include an updated LMN, an itemized receipt, corrected provider information, or a more detailed clinical rationale. If you're unsure what's needed, contact Truemed at support@truemed.com before reaching out to your administrator.

**Step 3: Contact your administrator to start the appeal**
Reach out to your HSA or FSA administrator directly to initiate the formal appeal. Ask about:
- The specific appeal deadline for your plan (this varies widely -- see the section below)
- The preferred method of submission (online portal, email, or mail)
- Any forms they require you to complete

**Step 4: Submit your updated documentation**
Submit your appeal with all updated materials within the deadline. Keep copies of everything you submit.

**Timeline expectations**
Most administrators respond to first-level appeals within 30 to 60 days. Some plans offer a second or even third level of appeal if the first is denied. FSA plans commonly allow up to three appeal rounds. HSA administrators (such as Lively or Fidelity) set their own policies -- submissions typically must occur within 180 days of denial, but confirm this with your specific institution.

## Re-submission process

Re-submitting a claim is slightly different from filing a formal appeal. A re-submission is appropriate when you have new or corrected documentation to add and want to try again before escalating.

**Can you submit more than once?**
In most cases, yes. If your claim was denied for a correctable reason (such as a missing signature or an incomplete receipt), you can re-submit after addressing the issue. Once a claim has been officially denied through the full appeals process, you generally must follow the formal appeal track rather than re-submitting as a new claim.

**What changes between submissions**
Each re-submission should specifically address the denial reason. Don't re-submit the same documentation -- update it to fix the identified problem. For example:
- If the denial was for a missing LMN signature, re-submit with a signed version
- If the receipt was missing line-item detail, re-submit with a fully itemized receipt
- If the diagnosis on the LMN didn't connect to the product, work with Truemed to update the clinical documentation

**Administrator timelines for re-submission**
Deadlines vary by administrator. A few examples:
- **Bank of America**: Typically 10 to 30 days to provide additional documentation after a denial
- **American Benefits Group (ABG)**: First-level appeals must be submitted within 180 days; expect a decision within 30 days
- **P&A Group**: Health Care FSA appeals within 180 days; Dependent Care FSA appeals within 60 days
- **HSA Bank**: Appeals must be submitted within 180 days; decisions typically within 60 days

Always confirm the current deadline directly with your administrator, as policies can change.

## When to contact support

**Contact Truemed when you need:**
- Help understanding why your claim was denied and what documentation is required to resolve it
- An updated or corrected Letter of Medical Necessity
- Clarification on whether a specific product qualifies for HSA/FSA reimbursement
- Assistance preparing documentation before re-submitting or appealing

To reach Truemed, email support@truemed.com with a screenshot of your denial notice, the stated denial reason or denial code, and your administrator's name. The support team will review your situation and recommend next steps.

**Go directly to your administrator when you need to:**
- File a formal appeal or re-submission
- Ask about your plan's specific deadlines or required forms
- Escalate a dispute beyond the documentation level
- Request an independent review (available under some FSA plans)

Truemed can help you prepare the right documentation and understand your options, but the formal appeal process is managed between you and your administrator.
