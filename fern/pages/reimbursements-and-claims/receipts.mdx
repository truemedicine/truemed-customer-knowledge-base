---
title: Receipts
description: How to find, understand, and submit your Truemed receipt for HSA or FSA reimbursement.
---

After you complete a Truemed-powered purchase and qualification, you will receive a receipt documenting your eligible expense. This receipt is one of the two documents you need to submit a successful reimbursement claim — the other being your Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN).

## How to access your Truemed receipt

Your Truemed receipt is delivered automatically after your purchase is qualified. Here is where to find it:

- **Check your email.** The receipt arrives from Truemed shortly after your qualification is complete. Search your inbox for an email from Truemed or look for a subject line that references your purchase or receipt. If you do not see it, check your spam or promotions folder.
- **Log into your Truemed dashboard.** If you have a Truemed account, you can access your receipt at any time by logging in and navigating to your purchase history. From there, you can view or download the receipt as a PDF.

Your Truemed receipt includes:

- The **merchant name** where the purchase was made
- The **product name and description**
- The **eligible amount** (the portion that qualifies for HSA/FSA reimbursement)
- The **date of purchase**
- Your **name** as the account holder

Keep this receipt on file. You will need it every time you submit a reimbursement claim, and your LMN is valid for 12 months — so you may use the same LMN with new receipts for additional qualifying purchases within that window.

## Receipt requirements for reimbursement

HSA and FSA administrators typically require that receipts contain specific information before they approve a reimbursement claim. The standard requirements are:

- **Provider or merchant name** — identifies where the purchase was made
- **Patient name** — must match the name on your LMN
- **Date of purchase** — confirms the transaction falls within your coverage period
- **Product or service name** — clearly identifies what was purchased
- **Product or service description** — explains what the item is and what it does

The description field is especially important. Administrators may flag receipts that use vague language like "membership," "program," or "class." Truemed's receipt includes detailed, plain-language descriptions that clearly explain what you purchased, which helps administrators recognize the item as an eligible medical expense.

If your administrator asks for something beyond what is on the Truemed receipt, see the section below on cost comparison documents, or contact Truemed support at support@truemed.com.

## Missing or unavailable receipt

If you cannot find your receipt, try the following steps in order:

1. **Check your email, including spam and promotions folders.** Receipts are sent automatically after qualification, so the email may have been filtered.
2. **Log into your Truemed dashboard.** Your receipt should be available there even if the email was lost or deleted.
3. **Contact Truemed support.** Email support@truemed.com and include your name, the merchant name, and the approximate purchase date. The support team can look up your transaction and resend your receipt.

If your purchase was made a long time ago, Truemed can still look up your record and reissue the receipt. There is no cutoff for requesting a copy. When you reach out, the more detail you can provide about the original purchase (merchant name, date, order number), the faster the team can locate it.

## Providing receipts to your TPA

Your third-party administrator (TPA) is the company that manages your HSA or FSA account. When you submit a reimbursement claim, you will typically need to provide both your Truemed receipt and your LMN together.

**How to submit:**

1. Download your Truemed receipt and your LMN (both are available from your Truemed dashboard or the emails Truemed sent you).
2. Log into your TPA's portal and navigate to the reimbursement or claims section.
3. Upload both documents. Some administrators have separate upload fields for the receipt and the LMN; others accept them as a combined attachment.
4. Submit your claim and save any confirmation number you receive.

**If your TPA asks for a cost comparison document:** Some administrators require a cost comparison when reviewing claims for HSA/FSA-eligible products like mattresses, treadmills, or other higher-cost items. This document shows the price difference between the standard version and the medically necessary version, confirming that the eligible amount reflects only the medically necessary portion of the cost.

For common products, Truemed automatically includes cost comparison documentation in your receipt or supporting documents. If you did not receive one and your administrator is requesting it, contact support@truemed.com with your order details and the Truemed team will provide the appropriate documentation.

Submitting your LMN, receipt, and any cost comparison document together in a single submission gives you the best chance of first-time approval and minimizes back-and-forth with your administrator.
