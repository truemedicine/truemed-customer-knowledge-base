When you are ready to submit a reimbursement claim for a Truemed purchase, you will use one of two paths: through a **Third Party Administrator (TPA)** or through your **healthcare plan** directly. Knowing which path applies to you saves time and avoids unnecessary back-and-forth.

## What is a TPA

A Third Party Administrator, or TPA, is a company that manages the administrative side of your health benefits on behalf of your employer or health plan. For HSA and FSA accounts, the TPA is the organization that holds your account, processes your claims, and sends you reimbursements.

TPAs are separate from your insurance company. Your health insurance might be through Aetna or Blue Cross, but your HSA or FSA could be administered by a completely different company.

Common TPAs that Truemed customers work with include:

- **Optum** (formerly Optum Financial / ConnectYourCare)
- **WEX** (formerly HealthEquity and WageWorks)
- **HSABank**
- **Navia Benefit Solutions**
- **Gusto**
- **Payflex**
- **Employee Benefits Corporation (EBC)**
- **Benefit Allocation System (BAS)**
- **JustWorks**
- **Basic**

Your TPA is typically shown on your benefits debit card or in your employer's benefits portal.

## TPA claim process

If your HSA or FSA is managed by a TPA, you will submit your reimbursement claim directly through them. Here is how the process generally works:

1. **Log into your TPA's portal or app.** Most TPAs have an online account where you can view your balance and submit claims.
2. **Find the "Submit a Claim" or "Reimbursement" section.** The exact label varies, but it is usually easy to find in the main navigation.
3. **Enter the expense details.** This includes the purchase date, merchant name, and dollar amount.
4. **Upload your documentation.** You will need both your Truemed Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) and your purchase receipt. Some TPAs accept them as a single combined upload; others have separate fields.
5. **Submit and save your confirmation.** Note any claim reference number for your records.

Each TPA's portal looks a little different, and some have quirks around which file types they accept or how they label fields. For step-by-step instructions specific to your administrator, see the [Submitting Your Claim by Administrator](/reimbursements-and-claims/submitting-your-claim-by-administrator) article.

## Healthcare claim process

Some people have benefits that are more tightly integrated with their health insurance plan rather than a standalone HSA or FSA debit card. In this case, the reimbursement process may route through your insurance carrier or employer health plan rather than a separate TPA.

This path is less common for Truemed reimbursements, but it can apply if:

- Your employer manages your FSA internally rather than through a third-party platform
- Your health plan includes an integrated spending account that is tied directly to your insurance coverage
- You are submitting a claim under an HRA that is administered by your insurance carrier

In these cases, you will typically submit your claim to your insurance company or HR department directly, following whatever process they have for eligible medical expenses. You will still need your Truemed LMN and receipt. Contact your HR team or benefits administrator if you are not sure where to submit.

## Which path applies to you

Here is a quick way to figure out which process to follow:

- **You have a standalone HSA or FSA debit card** (like a Visa or Mastercard with your administrator's name on it) -- this is almost always a TPA. Submit your claim through your TPA's portal.
- **Your benefits come through your employer's health plan and you do not have a separate debit card** -- you may be on the healthcare claim path. Check with your HR team or benefits portal.
- **You are not sure** -- look at your benefits card or log into your employer's benefits portal. The administrator's name is usually listed there. You can also ask your HR or benefits team: "Who administers my FSA or HSA?"

When in doubt, the TPA path is more common for Truemed customers. If you try to submit through a TPA and hit a dead end, reach out to support@truemed.com and we can help you figure out the right route.

## Common TPA questions

**How do I find out who my TPA is?**
Check the back of your benefits debit card -- there is usually a logo or name for the administrator. You can also log into your employer's benefits portal, or ask your HR or People Ops team directly.

**Can I submit multiple claims at once?**
Most TPA portals allow you to submit multiple claims, but each purchase typically needs its own separate claim with its own receipt. Your Truemed LMN covers multiple purchases within a 12-month period, so you only need one LMN -- just attach it to each individual claim you submit.

**What if my TPA does not recognize Truemed?**
Truemed provides a Letter of Medical Necessity issued by a licensed healthcare provider. TPAs do not need to "recognize" Truemed specifically -- they just need to see a valid LMN and receipt for an eligible medical expense. If your TPA pushes back, ask them to review the claim under IRS Publication 502. If you continue to have trouble, contact Truemed at support@truemed.com and we can help you navigate it.

**How long does TPA processing take?**
Most TPAs process claims within 7 to 10 business days. Some faster platforms can approve claims in as little as a few days; others may take up to two or three weeks depending on submission volume and how you submitted (online is faster than mail). If your claim has been pending for more than two weeks, follow up with your TPA's customer support team.
