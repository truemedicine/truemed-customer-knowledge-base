Truemed qualifies purchases as medically necessary for **HSA (Health Savings Account)** and **FSA (Flexible Spending Account)** reimbursement. This is not a health insurance claim.

## Truemed Works with HSA/FSA Accounts — Not Health Insurance

**Do not submit a Truemed reimbursement through your health insurance or healthcare provider portal.** Those systems handle medical billing — doctor visits, prescriptions, hospital stays. Submitting there will result in a denial, and that's expected. The right path is your HSA or FSA account, which is a separate account from your insurance.

---

## How to Find Your HSA or FSA Account

Your HSA or FSA is managed by a **third-party administrator (TPA)** — a company your employer or bank has partnered with to hold your account and process claims. Common administrators include Optum, WEX, HSABank, Gusto, Navia, Payflex, JustWorks, EBC, BAS, and Basic.

Here's how to identify yours:

- **Check your benefits debit card** — the logo or name on the card is usually your administrator (e.g., "Optum Financial," "WEX," "Payflex")
- **Check your employer benefits portal** — log into your company's HR platform (ADP, Gusto, Rippling, etc.) and look for a "Spending Accounts" or "Health Benefits" section
- **Ask HR** — your People Operations or HR team can confirm your administrator and share the portal link

---

## Am I in My Healthcare Portal or My HSA/FSA Portal?

This is one of the most common points of confusion — especially when the same company manages both your health insurance and your spending accounts.

**You're in your health insurance portal if:**

- The site shows claims for doctor visits, hospital stays, or prescriptions
- You see "Explanation of Benefits (EOB)" documents
- The account shows coverage limits, not a spendable dollar balance
- The portal belongs to Aetna, BlueCross BlueShield, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, or Humana

**You're in your HSA/FSA portal if:**

- You see a dollar balance you can spend or reimburse yourself from
- There's a "Reimburse Myself" or "Submit a Claim" option for out-of-pocket expenses
- The portal belongs to Optum Financial, WEX, HSABank, Payflex, Navia, or a similar benefits administrator

<Callout intent="warn">
Some companies manage both health insurance and HSA/FSA accounts under the same brand. If you have Optum health insurance and an Optum HSA, make sure you're logging into myoptumhealthfinancial.com — the HSA/FSA portal — not the insurance claims portal. These are separate logins even if they look similar.
</Callout>

## What Documents You'll Need

Every reimbursement submission requires two documents:

1. **Your Truemed Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN)** — download this from your [Truemed dashboard](https://app.truemed.com)
2. **Your purchase receipt** — from the merchant, showing the purchase date, item(s), and total amount paid

Some administrators (like Navia) require itemized receipts. Make sure your receipt clearly shows the date of purchase, item description, and amount.

---

## Submitting Your Claim

Once you've confirmed you're in your HSA/FSA portal:

1. Log into your administrator's portal or app
2. Navigate to **"Reimburse Myself"** or **"Submit a Claim"**
3. Enter the expense details — date, merchant name, and amount
4. Upload your **Truemed LMN** and your **purchase receipt**
5. Submit — most administrators process claims within 7–10 business days

For step-by-step instructions specific to your administrator, see the [Submitting Your Claim by Administrator](/reimbursements-and-claims/submitting-your-claim-by-administrator) guide.
