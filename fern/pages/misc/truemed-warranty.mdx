When a reimbursement doesn't go as planned, Truemed's warranty program is designed to have your back. This article explains what the warranty covers, how to file a claim, and what happens after a claim is approved -- including how your warranty check is issued.

---

## What the Truemed warranty covers

Truemed offers a warranty on qualifying purchases made through the Truemed platform when a reimbursement claim is denied by your HSA or FSA plan administrator and the issue cannot be resolved.

**What the warranty protects against**
The warranty is designed to protect you in situations where:
- Your Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) was rejected by your plan administrator
- Truemed attempted to resolve the denial on your behalf but was unable to get the claim approved
- The denial was not due to ineligibility of the account type (see exclusions below)

**Warranty amounts by purchase type**
The warranty amount depends on how your purchase was processed:
- Purchases made through Shopify checkout with Truemed: up to 40% of the original purchase price
- Purchases processed through merchant-paid qualification sessions: up to 30% of the original purchase price
- D2C qualification sessions: in most cases, the warranty covers the cost of the LMN fee, refunded directly

The total refund amount is discretionary and may vary based on the specifics of your case and purchase amount.

**Important exclusions**
The warranty does not apply in all circumstances. Claims may not be eligible if:
- Your account is an HRA (Health Reimbursement Arrangement) or a limited-purpose FSA -- these account types have restricted eligibility rules that fall outside what Truemed's warranty covers
- You are submitting to insurance rather than an HSA or FSA administrator
- The denial is the result of documentation that was not submitted or could not be corrected

**How to know if your purchase includes a warranty**
If you made a qualifying purchase through Truemed and your reimbursement claim has been denied, you may be eligible for the warranty. If you are unsure, contact our support team at support@truemed.com and we can review your situation.

## How to file a warranty claim

Filing a warranty claim starts with contacting Truemed's support team. Before you reach out, it helps to have a few things ready.

**Step 1: Gather your documentation**
Before submitting a warranty claim, collect the following:
- A copy of the denial letter or denial email from your HSA or FSA administrator, clearly showing the reason for denial
- A receipt showing the full purchase price and purchase date
- Your mailing address (warranty checks are physical checks mailed to you)
- The name of your HSA or FSA plan administrator

**Step 2: Contact Truemed support**
Email support@truemed.com with the subject line "Warranty Claim" and attach your documentation. Include a brief description of your situation -- what you purchased, when you submitted your claim, and what reason your administrator gave for the denial.

**Step 3: Truemed reviews your case**
Once your claim is submitted, our team will review your documentation and confirm whether a warranty payment applies. We may ask for additional information if needed, so keep an eye on your inbox.

**Step 4: Claim decision and next steps**
If your claim is approved, we will reach out to confirm the warranty amount and the mailing address for your check. If your claim is not approved, we will explain why and let you know if there are any other options.

## Warranty check issuance

When a warranty claim is approved, Truemed issues a physical check to you for the warranty amount.

**What is a warranty check?**
A warranty check is a paper check mailed to the address you provide. It represents a partial reimbursement of your original purchase price as defined by the warranty terms for your purchase type.

**How the check is delivered**
After your claim is approved and your mailing address is confirmed, Truemed processes the check through our payment system. The check is sent via standard mail to the address on file.

**Timeline from approval to issuance**
After your claim is approved, allow up to 10 business days for processing and mailing. Delivery time depends on standard postal timelines once the check has been sent.

**How to use the check**
Deposit or cash the check as you would any other paper check. There are no special steps required -- it functions as a standard check.

**What to do if your check does not arrive**
If you have not received your check within 3 weeks of being told it was processed, contact us at support@truemed.com. Include your name, the email address on your account, and a note that you are following up on a warranty check. We will look into the status and, if needed, issue a replacement.

## Denied warranty claims

Not every warranty request will be approved. Here is what you should know if your claim is denied and what steps you can take.

**Common reasons a warranty claim may be denied**

- **Ineligible account type**: If you have an HRA, a limited-purpose FSA, or are submitting to insurance, your account type falls outside the scope of Truemed's warranty
- **No overturn attempt on record**: Truemed's warranty process requires that at least one attempt was made to appeal or overturn the original denial with your administrator before a warranty is issued
- **Incomplete documentation**: If required documentation -- such as a denial letter, valid receipt, or active LMN -- is missing or does not cover the purchase in question, the claim may not be approved
- **The original purchase does not qualify**: In some cases, the purchase may not have been processed through a supported Truemed integration or may otherwise fall outside warranty eligibility

**What to do if your claim is denied**
If your warranty claim is denied, you will receive an explanation from our support team. Review the reason carefully. In some cases, you may be able to provide additional documentation or correct the issue and resubmit.

**Can you appeal a denied warranty claim?**
Yes. If you believe your claim was denied in error or if you can provide documentation that addresses the denial reason, reply to your denial notice or email support@truemed.com with the updated information. Our team will take another look.

**What helps make a stronger claim**
The more complete your documentation, the stronger your claim:
- A clear denial letter from your administrator showing the denial reason
- A full, itemized receipt with the merchant name, purchase date, and total amount
- An active LMN that covers the product category and the dates of your purchase
- A brief explanation of the situation in your own words

## Warranty claim timeline

Understanding the timeline helps you know when to expect a response and when to follow up.

**Typical processing time**

| Stage | Estimated time |
|---|---|
| Initial review after submission | 3 to 5 business days |
| Additional documentation requested (if needed) | Varies |
| Claim decision communicated | Within 7 to 10 business days of complete documentation |
| Check processing and mailing after approval | Up to 10 business days |
| Check delivery by mail | 5 to 10 additional business days depending on location |

**What can affect the timeline**
- Incomplete documentation at the time of submission will pause the review until everything is received
- High claim volume during certain periods may extend response times
- Verification of your mailing address is required before a check can be issued -- delays in confirming your address will delay issuance

**When to follow up**
If you have not heard back within 10 business days of submitting a complete claim, or if your check has not arrived within 3 weeks of being told it was issued, reach out to support@truemed.com with your name, email address, and a brief description of your case. We are happy to provide a status update.
