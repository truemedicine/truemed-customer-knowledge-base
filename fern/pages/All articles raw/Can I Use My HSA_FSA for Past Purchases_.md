## Understanding the Importance of LMN Dates

Your LMN is generated after you complete a clinical intake survey, which assesses your current medical condition. The LMN is dated based on when the survey is taken and reflects your condition at that specific point in time. This date is critical because:

- **Timeliness Matters:**
  The LMN must be dated on or before the day of your purchase to be valid for HSA/FSA reimbursement. For example, if you receive an LMN on March 1, it can only be used for expenses incurred on or after March 1.
- **Accuracy of Your Medical Condition:**
  The LMN documents your current health status and is based on an active patient-provider relationship. Backdating the LMN to cover past expenses (e.g., purchases made in January or February) would not accurately reflect your condition at that time.
***
## Why Past Purchases Aren't Eligible

If you made a purchase using a standard checkout instead of the TrueMed HSA/FSA checkout, the necessary clinical intake survey was not completed. This means:

- **No Valid LMN for That Purchase Date:**
  Without the survey, no LMN is issued, and there is no documentation to verify that your condition at the time of purchase warranted the expense.
- **Retroactive Reimbursement Isn't Permitted:**
  Even if you later obtain an LMN, you cannot change the LMN date to an earlier time because it must accurately represent your condition when the survey was completed. For instance, if you receive an LMN on March 1 but made purchases in January or February, you cannot retroactively apply that LMN because it does not reflect your condition during those months.
