## **\
Overview**

Truemed now supports **Split Pay**, allowing you to use multiple payment methods—such as an HSA/FSA card and a credit/debit card—for a single order. Whether your entire cart is HSA/FSA eligible or only certain items are eligible, this feature gives you the flexibility to split your payment in a way that best suits your needs.

## **Why Is This Important?**

- **Fully Eligible Carts**\
  If you have a large purchase or limited HSA/FSA funds, you can combine your HSA/FSA card with a credit/debit card to cover the rest. This helps you avoid maxing out your HSA/FSA card or worrying about insufficient funds.

- **Mixed Carts**\
  If your cart includes both eligible and non-eligible items, Split Pay lets you use your HSA/FSA card for eligible items and a credit/debit card for any remaining balance. This way, you won’t have to pay upfront and then seek reimbursement for the eligible portion.

## **How Does It Work?**

### **1. Checkout Screen Options**

Depending on whether your cart is entirely HSA/FSA eligible or mixed, you’ll see different payment options at checkout.

- **Fully Eligible Carts**

  1. Pay in full with HSA or FSA.

  2. Pay in full with credit or debit card (and later handle any reimbursement yourself, if needed).

  3. Split pay using HSA/FSA plus another card.

- **Mixed Carts**

  1. Pay in full with credit or debit card (and optionally seek reimbursement for the eligible items afterward).

  2. Split pay using HSA/FSA for the eligible portion and a credit/debit card for the rest.

  ![](https://cdn.applied.guide/media/38bb65d2-a9ef-4f17-b18d-241a04d41c06/81590925-76c3-48be-b69f-f1cd4e2e984b.png)

### **2. Splitting Your Payment**

If you choose to use two cards for a purchase, here’s what to expect:

1. **Enter Your HSA/FSA Card First**

   - You’ll see a field to enter how much you want to charge to your HSA/FSA card.

   - **Minimum Amount**: $0.50 (Stripe requires at least $0.50 on each payment method).

   - **Maximum Amount**: The eligible cost of the order (the HSA/FSA portion).

2. **Add Your Secondary Card**

   - After entering your HSA/FSA amount, you can add your credit or debit card.

   - This card will automatically cover the remaining balance of the order, which you **cannot** adjust independently (the HSA/FSA amount you entered determines how much the second card is charged).

3. **Review & Complete Payment**

   - You can still adjust the HSA/FSA amount before you complete payment.

   - If there’s an error (e.g., insufficient funds on your HSA/FSA card), you’ll see a message letting you know which card needs attention.

### **3. Payment Structure**

If you split payments, your purchase will be processed as **two separate payments**:

- One payment to your HSA/FSA card.

- One payment to your credit/debit card.

This ensures each card is charged only for the amount you designated (HSA/FSA) or whatever remains of your total.

## **Common Questions**

**1. What if my HSA/FSA card gets declined?**\
If your HSA/FSA card is declined, you’ll see an error message at checkout. You can adjust the amount you plan to place on your HSA/FSA card or use a different card.

**2. Can I pay the entire balance on my HSA/FSA if my cart is fully eligible?**\
Absolutely—if you have enough funds on your HSA/FSA card, simply choose “Pay in full with HSA or FSA.”

**3. How do I handle daily spending limits on my HSA/FSA card?**\
If you’re hitting a daily limit, just split your payment between your HSA/FSA card and a credit/debit card. The system will apply as much as allowed on your HSA/FSA card, and the rest will be charged to your secondary payment method.

**4. Do I need to reimburse myself if the cart is fully HSA/FSA eligible?**\
If you pay the entire amount on your HSA/FSA card, then you typically will not need to worry about reimbursement. If you only pay part of the amount on your HSA/FSA card and part on another card, you also won’t need to reimburse yourself for the HSA/FSA portion. However, if you pay fully with a credit/debit card, you may need to handle reimbursement on your own.
